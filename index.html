Independent Economics Research

Completed

Author · Research Paper

“To what extent has India’s recent shift toward trade protectionism affected social and income inequality?” — an approximately 13-page paper supported by academic literature and policy analysis.

  • Traced India’s trade trajectory and the liberalization-versus-protectionism debate.
  • Applied Adam Smith, Ricardo, Hamilton, and Friedrich List, and the theory of comparative advantage.
  • Analyzed the distributional consequences of tariffs and resulting social inequality.
  • Connected the analysis to modern Indian trade policy.

Tariff to Table

In Development

Interactive companion to the economics paper

An interactive website planned to accompany the trade-policy research. This project is in development and not yet complete.

  • A tariff calculator to estimate the effect of tariffs on household expenditure.
  • A Protectionism Simulator to experiment with tariffs, subsidies, trade agreements, and industrial policy — observing impacts on consumer prices, domestic industries, government revenue, and inequality.
